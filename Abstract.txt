Title: 
Proactive OpenADR Architecture via Digital Twin Sandboxing: A Critical Review and Proof of Concept for EV-Building Sector Coupling

Abstract:

The decarbonization of multi-energy systems necessitates advanced sector coupling, particularly integrating electric vehicle (EV) mobility with building thermal-electrical dynamics. While communication protocols like OpenADR facilitate Automated Demand Response (ADR), their traditional implementation remains inherently reactive, lacking proactive decision-support mechanisms to manage the complex trade-offs of local flexibility. This paper presents a critical, forward-looking review of Digital Twin (DT) applications for distributed flexibility, identifying a significant gap in real-time "what-if" simulation embedded within local energy management frameworks. To bridge this gap, we propose a novel proactive architecture based on "Digital Twin Sandboxing." By virtually replicating the local Energy Management System (EMS), the DT acts as a predictive oracle for the OpenADR Virtual End Node (VEN). Upon receiving a grid flexibility request from the Virtual Top Node (VTN), the DT simulates coordinated multi-vector strategies—concurrently modulating EV charging profiles and exploiting building thermal inertia via HVAC setpoints—prior to physical actuation. A Proof of Concept (PoC) validates this coordinated EV-building strategy, demonstrating that the DT-empowered sandboxing approach optimally satisfies grid operator constraints while strictly preserving occupant comfort and the required EV State of Charge (SoC). This hybrid review-methodological framework not only transitions local energy communities from reactive participants to proactive agents but also establishes a scalable, computationally efficient blueprint for the next generation of cognitive multi-energy microgrids.
